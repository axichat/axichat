Axichat is an open source SMTP (email) and XMPP (chat) client with 1st party push notifications (no FCM ever) and a conversation-focused interface.

Get your username@axi.im now or, for extra privacy and security, you can self-host your own email + XMPP server for Axichat by following the steps at https://github.com/axichat/selfhost.

Feature highlights:
- 1st-party push notifications and offline sync
- Unified inbox for chat + email
- Sync across all your devices (mobile and desktop)
- Group chats
- Avatars
- Easy forwarding and replying
- Emoji reactions
- Delivery and read receipts
- Typing indicators
- Attachments
- Pinned messages
- Stream management with automatic reconnect to stop messages dropping
- Easy drag-and-drop calendar
- Natural-language scheduling without AI
- Availability sharing that shows overlaps before you schedule
- One-tap add-to-calendar from simple text messages
- Guest mode for offline planning
- First-party push notifications and offline sync
- Works without Google, Firebase, and FCM

<strong>Note:</strong> Axichat is still under active development, so things may break.

If you find any bugs please report them at https://github.com/axichat/axichat/issues so I can fix them! GitLab also works for bug reports.

Why Axichat?
- Tools matter: communication should feel like a clean desk, not a cluttered inbox.
- Time matters: Axichat reduces context-switching with chat-style email and integrated planning.
- Collaboration matters: calendar, availability, and messaging stay in one place.

What Axichat does:

Beautiful chat interface:
- Read and send chat and email in one conversation-centric UI.
- Reach people on regular email providers (for example Gmail, Outlook, Tuta) while keeping your workflow in Axichat.
- Use advanced XMPP features when both sides support them.

Intuitive calendar:
- Type tasks and events in natural language and schedule quickly.
- Keep unscheduled items on the side until you place them.
- Plan with drag-and-drop, drag-to-resize, copy/paste, and batch editing.
- Track reminders, deadlines, priorities, and recurring plans.
- Use guest mode for local planning without account setup.

Privacy and independence:
- No trackers.
- No ads.
- No data selling.
- No vendor lock-in.
- Open protocols and open-source code.

Android permissions explained:
- INTERNET: send and receive chat/email data.
- POST_NOTIFICATIONS: show message and reminder notifications.
- FOREGROUND_SERVICE and related data-sync permissions: keep reliable background messaging.
- RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED: restore background messaging after reboot.
- REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS: optional prompt to improve delivery reliability.
- VIBRATE: notification haptics.
